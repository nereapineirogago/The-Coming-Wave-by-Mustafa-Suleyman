THE COMING WAY

by Mustafa Suleyman

Balancing Innovation, Risk, and Societal Control in an Era of Unprecedented Technological Change.

Part I: Homo Technologicus - Are We Really in Control?

Chapter 1: Containment is Not Possible

This chapter tackles the core argument: controlling or stopping the coming wave of technology appears impossible. It highlights the potential consequences if containment fails and emphasizes the need to confront this reality head-on, overcoming "pessimism aversion."

Chapter 2: Endless Proliferation

Tech spreads like wildfire.

From the printing press to smartphones, once the genie's out of the bottle...

Explores the historical pattern of technology, highlighting the mass diffusion in great waves. It uses the example of the internal combustion engine to illustrate how new general-purpose technologies proliferate and transform society. It also touches on whether societies have ever successfully said no to a new technology.

The question becomes, can any society truly say "no" to progress, even with potential downsides?

Chapter 3: The Containment Problem

Focuses on how to keep a grip on valuable technologies as they become cheaper and spread faster than ever before. The more valuable a technology, the harder it is to keep locked down.

How do we balance security with the benefits of widespread innovation?

Part II: The Next Wave - Riding the Crest of AI and Biology

Chapter 4: The Technology of Intelligence - The Rise of the Machines

Explores artificial intelligence as a general-purpose technology with immense promise and peril. It discusses the progress in AI, from systems that can learn valuable knowledge to large language models like ChatGPT. It emphasizes focusing on what AI systems can do, rather than getting caught up in debates about consciousness.

The crucial question: As AI gets smarter, how do we ensure it stays aligned with human values?

Chapter 5: The Technology of Life

Details synthetic biology as another general-purpose technology that is transforming our world. It highlights the rapid pace of change in biology, with mysteries unraveling and biology becoming an engineering tool. Synthetic biology lets us engineer living systems. Imagine custom-designed medicines or crops.

But what happens if these tools fall into the wrong hands?

Chapter 6: The Wider Wave

Explains that technological waves are bigger than just one or two general-purpose technologies. They are clusters of technologies arriving around the same time. It considers what comes after the coming wave, discussing technologies that will dominate the second half of the twenty-first century. It's not just AI and synthetic biology. Robotics, quantum computing, and other fields are merging to amplify the impact.

Are we prepared for the societal transformations and potential risks that lie ahead?

Chapter 7: Four Features of the Coming Wave

Four key features make this wave unique:

  • Omni-use: These technologies are general-purpose with a range of applications.
  • Hyper-evolution: The technologies evolve and improve rapidly.
  • Asymmetric Impacts: The benefits and risks are not evenly distributed.
  • Increasing Autonomy: They can increasingly operate without human control.

How can we ensure these technologies serve humanity and the planet?

Chapter 8: Unstoppable Incentives

Examines the macro-drivers behind technologies’ development and spread. It argues that technology emerges to fill human needs, driven by factors such as great power competition, a global research ecosystem, financial gains, the need to tackle global social challenges, and ego. Nations and corporations are racing to dominate these fields. It's a high-stakes game of security, wealth, and prestige.

How do we cooperate globally when everyone wants to win?

Part III: States of Failure - Will Our Institutions Survive?

Chapter 9: The Grand Bargain

Focuses on the consequences of the coming wave for the nation-state, particularly liberal democratic ones. It explains the bargain between the state and its citizens. It considers how the coming wave places this bargain under great threat. Nation-states provide security and services in exchange for power. But can they keep up with the coming wave?

In the face of technological advancements, how can nation-states adapt?

Chapter 10: Fragility Amplifiers

Focuses on applications and impacts of coming-wave technologies that will shake the already brittle foundations of the nation-state. These technologies can exploit existing vulnerabilities, from economic inequality to political polarisation.

How can we safeguard against cyberattacks, misinformation, and pandemics in an increasingly interconnected world?

Chapter 11: The Future of Nations

Considers a world after the coming wave, specifically the impact on the world's political order.

Will the rise of powerful corporations and AI-driven surveillance lead to a neo-feudal future, or can we find a path towards a more equitable distribution of power and resources?

Chapter 12: The Dilemma

Presents the central dilemma: grappling with the good and bad outcomes of continued openness versus confronting the dystopian and authoritarian risks of limiting technology proliferation.

Here's the crunch: Limit tech to control the risks, or embrace openness and risk disaster?

Part IV: Through the Wave - Navigating the Storm

Chapter 13: Containment Must Be Possible

Explores what containment might look like, even in theory, and emphasizes that regulation alone is not enough. It describes containment as a foundation for controlling and governing technology, encompassing technical, cultural, and regulatory aspects. Despite the challenges, we can't give up on control. Governance and ethical frameworks are key.

So, what specific actions can we take to create a safer, more ethical, and more controlled technological landscape?

Chapter 14: Ten Steps Toward Containment

Outlines ten areas of focus as necessary groundwork, emphasizing marginal gains and creating a different context for how technology is built and deployed. These ideas are presented as concentric circles, starting small and direct and getting progressively broader. These steps aim to create a new context for how technology is built and deployed.

  • Technical Safety
  • Audits
  • Choke Points
  • Makers
  • Businesses
  • Government
  • Alliances
  • Culture
  • Movements
  • Coherence

What are the most promising strategies for imposing constraints on technology, reforming governance, and fostering a healthier technological culture?

Life After the Anthropocene

The ultimate goal: Use technology to create a flourishing future for all. The more a technology by design requires human intervention, the less chance there is of losing control.

But it requires wisdom, foresight, and a commitment to shared values.

How can we ensure that technology amplifies human values and promotes the long-term flourishing of our species, rather than exacerbating existing inequalities and risks?
